Speaker FAQ

When and where do Tech Talks happen?

Location and Time will be announced shortly.

Who is welcome to speak?

Anyone! We give preference to TUM students and professors. But, we regularly host speakers from startups and tech companies. We like to mix things up!

What time should speakers arrive?

It is quite informal, so showing up 10-15 minutes before the talk is plenty of time.

What topic should I talk about?

The key word in "Tech Talk" is tech — we want technical topics that will be interesting to CS students. Talking about your weekend hack, side project, or CS research often makes a great Tech Talk. Focus on the technology — that's what people want to hear.

What is the time limit for each talk?

We cut speakers off after 30 minutes to keep things moving, but people are welcome to stick around afterward to talk to the speakers and each other.

Who will be in the audience?

ACM Tech Talks are open to the public, but the audience is predominantly TUM CS students.

What tools do presenters get?

We have a projector with VGA and HDMI input and adapters from MiniDP and DVI, a large whiteboard, and wireless network access. Let us know if you need anything else.
